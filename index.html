I. Introduction
The Core Purpose of This Session
Spiritual warfare and prophetic revelation have rarely been taught systematically in the church setting. Today we focus on one of two dark powers often discussed online but rarely properly addressed in church: Leviathan.

Discerning a Pattern, Not Hunting Witches

This session is not meant to train disciples to go around looking for “does this person have an evil spirit,” but to help us discern a spiritual pattern that recurs throughout Scripture.

Across the Personal, Family, and Church

This pattern shows up in personal life, in families, in the church — and also in the political realm, with an influence far beyond what we imagine.

Confusion Is Not Merely Interpersonal

When words are twisted, relationships torn, pride magnified, and confusion replaces peace, this may well be a spiritual-level attack, not simply a personality clash.

Positions Have Powers Behind Them

Political positions are only the outcome of a spiritual battle; every position has powers of influence behind it, and we need to be especially watchful in discerning this.

II. Biblical Basis (Part 1)
What Is Leviathan? — Job Chapter 41
The Chinese Union Version does not transliterate “Leviathan,” so many verses that mention it go unnoticed by Chinese readers. The English Bible, however, lists the word clearly.
  • Its scales are “shut up together as with a close seal … joined one to another, they stick together, that they cannot be sundered”
  • It “beholdeth all high things: he is a king over all the children of pride”
  • These descriptions go far beyond an ordinary crocodile — a being that commands the sea
  • Leviathan is “a king over all the children of pride” — not an ordinary creature

The Core of Leviathan
A King Over All the Children of Pride
Job 41:34
Leviathan is called “the king of all the children of pride” — this is the key to understanding it: its essence is pride. When discerning a spiritual attack, ask: what is its nature? Uncleanness? Greed? Pride? Fear? Every dark power's character matches its name — and Leviathan's core is pride.
Testimony reference: When a certain Satanic cult leader in America was struck down by the Lord, a demon appeared to a pastor and, when asked who it was, answered: “I am Leviathan” — confirming it as a real, high-ranking spiritual power.

Key Details
Key One: Humility  /  Key Two: Bless Instead of Accuse

Humility

Wherever pride reigns is fertile ground for Leviathan; the more a person humbles themselves, the weaker its influence. When facing conflict, examine yourself first:

  • Is there pride in me?
  • Is there excessive defensiveness in me?
  • Is there a refusal to be corrected in me?
  • Do I treat my feelings as absolute truth?
  • Am I using spiritual language to cover my hurt?

Bless Instead of Accuse

When others reject, misunderstand, or slander us, the natural response is to defend, attack back, and prove we're right — but this only leads to a vicious cycle. We must learn to bless those who curse us, cutting off the cycle and guarding our hearts.

When the fight can't get started, Leviathan has nothing to work with.

Key Details
Keys Three to Five

Stop Feeding the Fire

  • Don't forward unverified information
  • Don't escalate conflicts or add fuel
  • Don't spread spiritual gossip or every word

Restore Clarity Through Truth

  • Return to facts, Scripture, face-to-face talk
  • Don't speculate on others' motives
  • Don't let third parties dictate the relationship

Reject Spiritual Pride

  • “I heard from God, I don't need testing”
  • “I'm more spiritual than my leader”
  • A prophet is human, and needs to be tested
